Where to Buy a Watch in Tirana, Albania

An honest look at your options in Tirana — shopping centres, jewellers, and online — plus why many Tirana residents make the 35-minute drive to Iglisi Watch in Durrës for something worth keeping.

5 min read Iglisi Watch — Durrës April 2026

The Tirana Watch Market: What’s Actually Available

Tirana is Albania’s capital and its largest city, and it has more watch retail options than anywhere else in the country. That said, “more options” does not automatically mean “better options” — particularly if you are looking for something in the €55–200 range with a proper warranty and somewhere to come back to if anything needs attention.

Here is an honest breakdown of where people in Tirana buy watches, and what each option actually delivers.

Your Options in Tirana

Shopping centres (QTU, Tirana East Gate, TEG)

The large malls carry international fashion brands and a handful of watch retailers. You will find brands like Casio, Fossil, and occasionally Tissot or Longines at the premium end. The advantage is familiarity and a formal retail environment. The disadvantage: most of these shops do not have an on-site watchmaker, so any repair or battery change means coming back or sending the watch away. For everyday watches under €200, prices tend to be higher than buying direct from a specialist.

Jewellery shops (Rruga Kavajës, Blloku area)

Many jewellery shops along Rruga Kavajës and in the Blloku district carry watches as a secondary line. Quality varies considerably. Some carry genuine branded stock with warranty documentation; others carry items from generic wholesalers where provenance is unclear. The key question to always ask: “Can you service this watch here, or do I need to take it elsewhere?” Most jewellery shops in Tirana are not equipped for watch servicing beyond basic strap swaps.

Online (international retailers)

For established brands — Seiko, Orient, Casio G-Shock, Tissot — buying online from the brand’s own website or a verified European retailer is entirely reasonable. The complications arise with returns (Albania is outside the EU, which complicates warranty claims), shipping times, and the fact that you still need a local watchmaker for any future battery change, strap fitting, or servicing. Factor that cost in when comparing prices.

Street vendors and bazaars

Avoid. Watches sold by street vendors in Tirana are almost universally replicas or very low-quality unbranded pieces. There is no warranty, no return mechanism, and no one to contact when the watch stops working. The repair cost invariably exceeds what the watch is worth. If you have been sold one unknowingly, bring it to any watchmaker for a free authenticity check.

Why a Specialist Watchmaker Is Different

The main advantage of buying from a watchmaker rather than a jewellery shop or mall retailer is that the same person who sells you the watch can also maintain it. When the battery needs changing in three years, you come back to the same place. When the crown gets stiff or the strap starts to fray, you have a workshop, not a customer service desk.

At Iglisi Watch we stock only brands we have used and serviced ourselves — Hislon and Navimarine. We know exactly how these movements behave, how long the batteries last, and what to expect over years of daily wear. That is not something a mall retail assistant can tell you.

A note on replica watches. A significant volume of counterfeit watches circulates in Albanian markets, including in Tirana. These are illegal to sell and import, use very short-lived movements, and cannot be repaired because genuine parts do not exist. If you are offered a watch at a price that seems implausibly low for the stated brand, it is almost certainly a replica. Bring it to any watchmaker for a free check — it takes less than a minute to confirm.
