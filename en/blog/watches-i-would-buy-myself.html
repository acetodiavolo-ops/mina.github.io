The 5 Watches I Would Buy From My Own Shop

Customers ask me this all the time: forget the sales talk, which ones would you actually buy? After years at this bench, here are the five I would spend my own money on, and the honest reason for each.

7 min read Iglisi Watch - Durrës July 2026

There is a difference between the watch I sell you and the watch I would buy myself, and a shop worth trusting should be honest about it. So here is my personal list: five watches from our own shelves that I would happily pay for with my own money. They are not the most expensive things we stock, and that is the point. A good watch is the one that suits your life, not the one with the biggest number on the tag. Here is what I would pick, and why.

1. The One I Actually Wear

The Navimarine Steel 5ATM Blue is on my wrist most days, and it is the first watch I hand to anyone who wants one watch that does everything. Stainless steel, sapphire crystal, a genuine 5 ATM water rating, and a blue sunburst dial that looks as good with a shirt as with a t-shirt. It swims, it survives the workshop, and it never feels like a compromise.

Navimarine Steel 5ATM Blue €75 / 7,300 Lek

Sapphire, steel, 5 ATM, the do-everything daily. If you only read one more thing, read our summer watch guide, where this is the star pick for the beach.

2. The Gift I Hand Over Most

When someone needs a watch that looks like a proper present without a proper-present price, I reach for the Daniel Klein Premium. Gold-tone case, silver sunburst dial, a Datejust-inspired profile that reads far above its €64. It is a fashion watch and I say so openly, but for the money nothing else in the shop presents this well in a box.

Daniel Klein Premium €64 / 6,200 Lek

The most look for the fewest euros, and my go-to gift. The full honest picture is in our Daniel Klein review: Turkish fashion brand, quartz, gold-tone plating, worth it for what it is.

3. When I Want a Bit of Colour

Not every day is a plain-dial day. The Navimarine NAVI 009-COL 6 is the one I would pick when I want the watch to say something. Bold colourful dial, solid steel case, sapphire crystal, and it still keeps quiet quartz reliability underneath the personality. It is the most fun watch on my list.

Navimarine NAVI 009-COL 6 €95 / 9,200 Lek

The colour statement, still built like a tool watch. It tops the Navimarine sport range, which I break down in the price ladder.

4. The One That Looks Like More Than It Costs

The Hislon Masterwork chronograph is the watch people assume cost me three times what it did. Jet-black dial, three subdials, sapphire crystal, steel bracelet, red accents. It is sport-chic, it dresses up and down, and the chronograph gives your eye something to enjoy. At €184 it is the piece I would wear when I want to look sharp.

Hislon Masterwork chronograph €184 / 17,800 Lek

The sharpest watch for the money in the shop. See where it sits against the cheaper tiers in the price ladder.

5. The One I Would Keep Forever

If I could keep only one watch to pass on one day, it would be the Hislon Classic CL213S-02SS. Clean stainless steel, sapphire crystal, a striped silver dial and a slim profile that will never look dated. It is a proper dress watch that does not shout, the kind you wear for twenty years and hand to someone who will wear it for twenty more.

Hislon Classic CL213S-02SS €199 / 19,300 Lek

My keep-forever pick. Read the full Hislon Classic review, and if you are shopping as a couple, it anchors one of the sets in our matching watches guide.

The honest summary. If you want one watch that does everything, the Navimarine Steel 5ATM Blue. The best gift under €80, the Daniel Klein Premium. A statement, the NAVI 009. To look sharp, the Hislon Masterwork. To keep forever, the Hislon Classic. Every one is in stock in Durrës, quartz, with a 1-year guarantee. That is the whole list, no filler.
