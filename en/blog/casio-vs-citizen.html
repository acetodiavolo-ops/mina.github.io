Casio vs Citizen Watch: Which Is Worth Buying?

Two Japanese giants, one price range, and very different reasons to buy either. Here’s an honest, detailed comparison of the Casio MTP-VD01 and the Citizen Quartz Gold - so you can decide with confidence.

8 min read Iglisi Watch - Durrës May 2026

Two Japanese Brands, One Question: Which Do You Actually Need?

Casio and Citizen are both Japanese watchmaking institutions, and both are sold at Iglisi Watch in Durrës. But “both Japanese” is where the similarities end. Casio built its reputation on bold, functional engineering - a brand that gave the world the G-Shock, the F-91W, and generations of reliable sport watches. Citizen built its reputation on horological precision, crafting movements that hold their own against Swiss competitors and pioneering technology like Eco-Drive solar charging.

At the models we stock - the Casio MTP-VD01 at €52 and the Citizen Quartz Gold at €60 - both watches sit in the same approachable price bracket. But they are built for different wrists and different wardrobes. This comparison will tell you exactly who should buy each one.

Casio MTP-VD01 - Built for the Real World

The Casio MTP-VD01 (reference MTP-VD01D-1EVUDF) is what happens when a brand with 80 years of engineering culture makes a no-compromise everyday watch at an honest price. The first thing you notice is the dial: a deep matte black with silver baton indices that catch the light cleanly, luminous coating on both hands and markers, and a crisp date window at 3 o’clock. It is a watch that is immediately legible in any light condition.

The case and bracelet are stainless steel throughout - not a compromise finish, not a plated bezel. The bracelet has a folded construction that is comfortable and carries well on the wrist. Water resistance is rated at 50 metres, which means it handles daily life without anxiety: rain, hand-washing, a splash at the beach. The Japanese quartz movement inside is accurate to within ±15 seconds per month, which is better than most buyers will ever need.

What Casio has done with this model is compress genuine value into a watch that retails at €52. The MTP-VD01 does not pretend to be a luxury watch, but it gives you something more useful: a honest, durable everyday piece that earns its keep.

Casio MTP-VD01 - Strengths

  • 50m water resistance - genuinely useful for everyday life
  • Luminous baton indices for easy reading in low light
  • Date window at 3 o’clock - practical, not decorative
  • Full stainless steel case and bracelet
  • Accurate Japanese quartz movement
  • Versatile black dial wears with casual and smart-casual outfits
  • Sharp entry-level price of €52 (5,000 L)

Casio MTP-VD01 - Limitations

  • Mineral glass crystal scratches more easily than sapphire
  • Bold, sporty character makes it less suited to formal dress occasions
  • Bracelet clasp is functional rather than refined
  • Limited collector prestige compared to higher Casio lines

Citizen Quartz Gold - Heritage and Elegance

Citizen has been making watches since 1918 and holds a genuine place in the history of horology. The brand pioneered the first Japanese-made pocket watch and later led the world in quartz movement development. The Citizen Quartz Gold watch at our shop carries that heritage quietly - it is not a watch that shouts its credentials, but the quality of its construction is evident the moment you hold it.

The gold-tone case and matching bracelet give it a warm, dressed finish that reads as classic elegance. The champagne dial is refined: clean, uncluttered, with gold-toned hour markers and hands that maintain perfect legibility without resorting to high contrast or sport-style indices. The “CITIZEN QUARTZ” lettering across the dial is understated but adds a brand presence that wearers often appreciate.

At €60, the Citizen Quartz Gold sits just above the Casio on price, but it offers something genuinely different: a watch you could wear with a suit or a formal dress without any sense that you are underdressed. It is a watch designed to be worn on important occasions and forgotten about in the best possible way.

Citizen Quartz Gold - Strengths

  • Classic gold-tone aesthetic that suits formal and semi-formal occasions
  • Refined champagne dial - clean, legible, and elegant
  • Strong brand heritage and global recognition
  • Gold-tone bracelet and case make it a natural gift choice
  • Accurate Japanese quartz movement with Citizen’s precision reputation
  • A versatile dress watch at an accessible price of €60 (5,800 L)

Citizen Quartz Gold - Limitations

  • Gold-tone finish is plated, not solid gold - can show wear over years
  • Less practical for active use or outdoor wear
  • No date window on this model
  • Gold styling is classic but polarising - not for everyone

Who Should Buy the Casio MTP-VD01?

You want one watch that survives everyday life

With 50m water resistance, a tough stainless steel construction, and luminous indices for early mornings or late evenings, the MTP-VD01 is built to be put on and forgotten about. It handles the daily wear that a dress watch would not.

You need to know the date at a glance

The date window at 3 o’clock sounds like a small thing until you miss it on a watch that lacks one. It is genuinely useful for anyone who works in a setting where quick date checks matter.

You want a strong first watch or gift on a budget

At €52, the Casio MTP-VD01 punches well above its price for features and build. It is a compelling first watch for a teenager or young adult, and an honest gift that will be used rather than stored.

Your style is more casual or smart-casual

The black dial and stainless steel combination works well with jeans, a leather jacket, or business-casual attire. If your life happens mostly outside a formal context, the Casio fits that register naturally.

Who Should Buy the Citizen Quartz Gold?

You work in a formal environment

The Citizen Quartz Gold’s warm gold-tone and champagne dial belong in meetings, at dinners, and on any occasion where your appearance matters. It reads as a considered, classic choice.

You are buying a gift for a milestone occasion

A gold-toned Citizen with a champagne dial has gift energy that a sporty stainless steel watch does not. For graduations, anniversaries, birthdays, and celebrations, the Citizen reads as a meaningful, elevated choice.

You want a recognisable brand with a long history

Citizen has been making watches for over 100 years. Wearing a Citizen says something about your appreciation for watchmaking tradition. For someone who values provenance and brand story, Citizen delivers that at an accessible price.

You prefer a watch that works across multiple wardrobes

A gold-tone dress watch is more transferable across formal contexts than a sport watch. If you wear suits to work, smart casual in the evening, and formal at weekends, the Citizen can hold all three roles without looking out of place.

The Same Movement, Different Worlds

One thing worth understanding before you buy: both the Casio and the Citizen in this comparison use Japanese quartz movements, and both will keep time to a similarly high standard - expect accuracy within 10–20 seconds per month at most. The real differences are not in the timekeeping but in everything around it: the materials, the design language, the features, and the impression the watch makes on your wrist and on others.

Casio’s movement engineering has a sport heritage. The MTP-VD01 movement is calibrated for reliability under daily wear conditions, with a battery life measured in years. Citizen’s quartz tradition goes back to the early 1970s, when the brand was competing directly with Swiss manufacturers to produce the most accurate movements in the world. The Citizen name on a dial still carries that provenance.

Price and Value: Who Wins?

The Casio MTP-VD01 at €52 and the Citizen Quartz Gold at €60 are separated by €8. That is not a meaningful financial decision - the real question is what you get for each. The Casio gives you a bigger feature set: water resistance to 50m, luminous hands and indices, and a date display. The Citizen gives you a more refined finish: the warmth of gold toning, a cleaner dial aesthetic, and more than a century of brand heritage.

Our practical take: If you are buying for function and everyday durability, the Casio is the smarter €52 spend. If you are buying for occasion, as a gift, or because you wear formal attire regularly, the extra €8 for the Citizen is money well spent. Both are in stock at our Durrës shop - see the Casio MTP-VD01 listing and the Citizen Quartz Gold listing.

The Honest Verdict

Choose the Casio MTP-VD01 if: you want a practical, durable everyday watch with useful features (date, lume, water resistance), you prefer a bold stainless steel look that suits casual and smart-casual wear, and €52 feels like the right price for what you get - which it genuinely is.

Choose the Citizen Quartz Gold if: you want a classic dress watch with gold-tone elegance, you are buying for a formal occasion or as a meaningful gift, and you appreciate owning a piece from a brand with over 100 years of watchmaking history behind it.

If you are genuinely torn, consider the context you live in. Watches that stay in a drawer because they feel “too nice” for everyday use have less value than watches that get worn. The Casio is the one more people will actually put on every morning. The Citizen is the one you will reach for when it matters.
