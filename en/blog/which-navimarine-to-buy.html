Which Navimarine Should You Buy?

The Navimarine range covers sport, classic, and chronograph styles at different price points. A guide to matching the right model to your lifestyle.

9 min read Iglisi Watch - Durrës May 2026

The Navimarine Range

Navimarine produces watches in three broad styles: sport, classic, and chronograph. They are all quartz, all stainless steel, and all in the €60–120 range. The differences between them are real and matter for daily use, so it is worth understanding what each category offers before choosing.

Sport Models: Built for Use

The Navimarine sport range is the core of the brand. Case diameters of 42–45mm, high water resistance, bold indices with applied lume, and rubber or silicone straps. These are watches designed for people who use their watch rather than just wearing it.

The dial layouts are busier than the classic models. Multiple track rings, prominent indices, and in some cases a 24-hour bezel or rotating outer scale. That adds visual interest and practical function. If you like a watch that looks like it does something, this is the category.

Water resistance on the sport models is typically 100m or higher, which is sufficient for swimming, snorkelling, and beach use. Not for scuba diving, but more than adequate for everyday active wear near water.

Classic Models: Versatile Everyday Pieces

The Navimarine classic range is more restrained. Cases around 38–42mm, cleaner dials with fewer elements, and leather or steel bracelet options alongside rubber. These are watches that work in casual and semi-formal settings without the sport visual weight.

If you want a Navimarine for everyday office wear rather than outdoor use, or for someone who wants a reliable watch that is not obviously a sport piece, the classic range is the better fit. Water resistance is lower than the sport models, typically 30–50m, which covers splashing and daily wear but not swimming.

Chronograph Models: More Dial, More Interest

The Navimarine chronograph-style pieces have three subdials and a more complex dial layout. The stopwatch function may or may not be the point; for most buyers, the appeal is the visual complexity of the dial. These read as more serious watches on the wrist.

Case sizes on the chronograph line are typically 44–46mm, so they suit larger wrists or people who prefer a strong presence on the wrist. Not for everyone. But for someone who wants a watch with real visual impact in the sub-€100 range, these are hard to match.

Active and Outdoor Lifestyle

Sport model, 100m water resistance, 42–44mm case. Buy the rubber strap variant for comfort during physical activity.

Everyday Casual to Semi-Formal Wear

Classic model, 38–42mm, leather strap or steel bracelet. Clean dial. Works Monday to Sunday without looking out of place anywhere.

Want Maximum Dial Interest Under €100

Chronograph model. Three subdials, bold case, strong wrist presence. Perfect for people who want a visually complex watch without spending more than is necessary.

Buying as a Gift

The classic model is the safest gift choice in the Navimarine range. Clean, versatile, wearable by almost anyone. If you know the person is active, go sport. If they prefer a more complex-looking watch, go chronograph.

On strap choice. Most Navimarine models are available with rubber, leather, or steel bracelet strap options. Rubber is the most practical for active use. Leather looks better in casual and semi-formal settings but requires more care. Steel bracelet sits in the middle: durable, adjustable, and works across most contexts.

What to Check Before Buying

Case diameter: above 42mm is bold and suits larger wrists. Below 40mm is more universal. Check the product listing. Water resistance: if you are near water regularly, confirm the model is rated to at least 100m. Strap type: buy for how you actually live, not how you wish you lived. A rubber strap on a watch you wear in an office will look out of place within a week.

Navimarine for Women

The Navimarine range is predominantly male-oriented in case size - the sport and chronograph models at 42–45mm are too large for most women’s wrists. The classic range includes models down to 36–38mm that work well as women’s watches. These smaller-cased classics have cleaner dials, more refined proportions, and in some cases a two-tone finish (steel case with gold-tone accents) that suits a more dress-forward context.

For women buying Navimarine, the classic range at the smaller end is the right starting point. Ask us on WhatsApp for which models are currently in stock below 38mm and we can photograph them for you.

Navimarine as a Gift

Navimarine is a practical gift choice for several reasons. The price range (€60–€120) is generous without being extravagant. The design is legible and widely wearable, not so distinctive that it only suits one type of person. And the stainless steel construction means the watch will hold up to daily wear without looking tired within a few years.

For a graduation, name day, or birthday gift where you do not know the recipient’s taste precisely, a Navimarine classic in the €80–€100 range with a leather strap is a safe and considered choice. For someone with an active lifestyle, a sport model with rubber strap and 100m water resistance is more appropriate.
