What a Quartz Watch Service Includes

A quartz watch that keeps losing more and more time, stops and restarts, or shows an irregular seconds hand almost never just needs a new battery - those are signs the movement needs attention. A full quartz service includes:

  • Full movement disassembly: removal from the case and separation of every gear-train component
  • Ultrasonic cleaning: all parts are cleaned in an ultrasonic bath with a dedicated solution, removing accumulated dirt, hardened old oil and surface oxidation
  • Selective lubrication: every wheel, pinion and contact point is re-oiled with the correct lubricant for the movement type
  • Battery replacement: after cleaning we always fit a fresh, quality cell matched to the original reference
  • Testing and regulation: the movement is tested for at least 24 hours before return to confirm accuracy
  • Gasket check: the water-resistance seal is inspected and replaced if needed

How to tell you need a service: If your quartz watch loses more than 15 seconds a day on a fresh battery, or stops and restarts for no obvious reason, it’s almost always the movement that needs attention - not the battery.

What a Mechanical Watch Service Includes

A mechanical or automatic watch that loses significant time, whose balance stops, or that makes unusual noises needs a full service. Under normal conditions, mechanical movements need servicing every 5–7 years. A mechanical service includes everything listed for quartz, plus:

  • Complete movement strip-down: every wheel, spring and lever is removed, catalogued and inspected individually under magnification
  • Mainspring inspection: the spring that stores the energy is checked for cracks, deformation or excessive wear
  • Multi-stage professional cleaning: an ultrasonic bath followed by multiple rinses to remove every trace of old oil
  • Reassembly with specific lubricants: each lubrication point receives the correct type and amount of oil for its function
  • Balance regulation: the oscillation rate is measured on timing equipment and adjusted for the best accuracy the movement allows
  • Full wind-and-run test: the watch is fully wound and monitored over several days to verify power reserve and accuracy over time
